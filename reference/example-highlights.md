# RAC Intranet Story Highlights — Reference Examples

**Purpose:** Examples of real published RAC Intranet highlights, used as reference for the `intranet-post` skill when generating highlights for submissions where the submitter left the Highlight field blank.

**How to use this file:** The highlight is *the voice*. While the title states the topic, the highlight conveys the *feeling* — what makes this story matter to the reader. Match the energy and the personality, not the exact phrasing.

---

## Modal Stories tab

### `Tripod clean sweeps the Golf Club Trivia Night 💪`
*Type:* Win recap
*What works:* Specific verb phrase ("clean sweeps") + concrete location ("Golf Club Trivia Night") + emoji punctuation. Reads with energy. The flexed-bicep emoji is celebratory without being childish.
*Length:* 9 words + emoji.

### `Photos from the event at the town hall.`
*Type:* Event documentation
*What works:* Plain, factual, low-key. Sometimes the highlight just signposts what's in the story. Not every highlight needs to sparkle.
*Length:* 8 words.

### `It's a Girl!!!`
*Type:* Personal milestone
*What works:* Three exclamation marks earn their keep here. Joy is appropriate; restraint would be wrong. Don't pull punches on genuinely happy news.
*Length:* 3 words.

### `Exciting Restructure for 2026`
*Type:* Operational change
*What works:* Frames a potentially nervous topic ("restructure") positively without dismissing weight. "Exciting" is a deliberate signal: this is opportunity, not threat.
*Length:* 4 words.

### `Great official launch of Yanawal Kitchen. Well received 'The Road Ahead' event hosted and key messages on positivity and Land Tenure decisions ahead delivered along with compelling speeches by our Management.`
*Type:* Multi-part event recap
*What works:* Edge case — sometimes a story has multiple components and the highlight tries to capture all of them. Works but borderline. Skill should aim shorter than this most of the time.
*Length:* 31 words. (Long for a highlight — aim shorter.)

### `She's a beauty!`
*Type:* Project / object reveal
*What works:* Australian vernacular at its finest. Three words convey pride, warmth, informality. Works for kitchens, vehicles, buildings, anything you'd point at.
*Length:* 3 words.

### `Team photo`
*Type:* Photo-only story
*What works:* Sometimes the highlight is just a label. Don't manufacture poetry where none is needed.
*Length:* 2 words.

## Manager-tier tabs

### `This year's AGM was a great opportunity for members to come together, stay informed, and hear about all the exciting work underway across the corporation.` *(CEO Messages)*
*Type:* CEO recap
*What works:* Slightly more formal register, complete sentence, balanced cadence ("come together, stay informed, and hear..."). Lands warmth without slipping into corporate-speak. Notice it's still recognisably RAC voice — not "leveraging synergies."
*Length:* 25 words.

### `Let's talk about what we can do better!` *(Business Messages)*
*Type:* Workshop / collaboration
*What works:* Inclusive ("Let's"), forward-looking ("what we can do better"), exclamation conveys momentum without being saccharine. Inviting, not corrective.
*Length:* 8 words.

### `Making headway at Bunggulwuy. Blockwork is almost complete. Sparkies and Plumbers are roughing in.` *(Operations Messages)*
*Type:* Site progress update
*What works:* Three short sentences. Trade vernacular ("Sparkies", "roughing in") signals authenticity — written by someone who's actually on the site, not by a marketing team. Concrete, no fluff.
*Length:* 14 words across 3 sentences.

### `Last week, local youth music group, Lucky Band, laid down vocal tracks for their soon to be released music video titled "Who we are" with coaching from Break'n Barrierz mentor Maggie Garawirrtja.` *(Community Messages)*
*Type:* Community recognition
*What works:* News-brief format, names everyone (group, song title, mentor, mentor's organisation). Specific. Edge case — quite long for a highlight (the title carries less detail because the highlight is doing more work).
*Length:* 32 words. (Borderline long.)

### `Safety is everyone's business` *(Safety Messages)*
*Type:* Campaign / theme
*What works:* Inclusive framing. Three words, complete thought, no exhortation. Safety messaging that doesn't lecture — pulls people in rather than pushing them.
*Length:* 4 words.

## Patterns at a glance

**Length:** 3–15 words is the sweet spot. Some are longer (event recaps, news briefs), but if you're drifting toward 30 words, ask whether the highlight is doing too much.

**Tone:** Warm, specific, confident. Doesn't oversell. Doesn't undersell.

**Punctuation:** Exclamation marks are welcome where joy or pride is genuine. Multiple exclamations only for *real* milestones (births, big wins). Periods at the end optional — many highlights work without them. Question marks rare.

**Emojis:** One at the end is fine. Two is the absolute maximum. Skill should pick from these tonal cues: 💪 (win/strength), 🏆 (achievement), 💕 (warmth/family), 🎤 (music/audio), 🤓 (geeky-fun), ✌🏾 (community/peace), 🎥 (video). **Never use** emojis that read as ironic or sarcastic in this context.

**Vocabulary:** Australian/Yolŋu vernacular is positively encouraged where it fits. "Sparkies" beats "electricians". "She's a beauty" beats "It looks excellent". "Mob" used naturally, never explained.

**Names:** When the story has a hero — a person, project, team — name them in the highlight. Specificity beats abstraction.

## Highlights for thin signal

Sometimes the submission has minimal text — three photos and "team day at the kitchen". The skill still has to write a highlight. In these cases:

- **Don't fabricate details.** If you don't know what happened, don't invent a narrative.
- **Lean on what's there.** "Team day at the kitchen" → "Team day at Yanawal Kitchen" if context allows, or just "Great day with the team at the kitchen 💪".
- **Or just label.** "Team photo" is a valid highlight when there's nothing else to say.

If the highlight feels forced or padded, it is. Trust the photos and body to do the work.

---

## What to avoid

- **Corporate hype words:** "Outstanding", "world-class", "best-in-class", "synergistic". These are tells of bot-written content.
- **Vague feel-good filler:** "It was a great day for everyone involved." Empty calories.
- **SEO-style framing:** "Discover how RAC...", "5 reasons why...", "Everything you need to know about..."
- **Begging for engagement:** "What do you think? Comment below!" — the Intranet doesn't have comments.
- **Apology framing:** "Sorry for the long delay but..." — submitters already have enough friction; the skill shouldn't add self-deprecation.
- **Over-claiming:** "Historic moment", "unprecedented success" — only if it genuinely is. Otherwise dial it down.

---

## Notes for the skill

When generating a highlight:

1. **Start by asking: what's the *feeling* here?** Pride? Joy? Welcome? Progress? Concern (in safety messages)? Match the feeling to the energy of the highlight.
2. **Find the most concrete detail.** A name, a place, a number, a verb. Build around that.
3. **Aim for 5–12 words.** Longer is fine if the story genuinely needs it (multi-event recaps), but the default should be short.
4. **One emoji at most, at the end.** Only if it adds real meaning. Skip if uncertain.
5. **Read it aloud.** If it sounds like a press release or a LinkedIn post, redo it. RAC highlights sound like a person — usually a friendly, slightly proud one.
6. **Stop and check:** if this highlight appeared next to "She's a beauty!" or "Tripod clean sweeps the Golf Club Trivia Night 💪", would it look out of place? If yes, adjust.
